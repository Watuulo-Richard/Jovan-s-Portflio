About Me

I am Lubega Jovan, a Senior Statistician at the Ministry of Education and Sports in Uganda.

With over 10 years of experience in the field of statistics and data analysis. Throughout my career, I have been fortunate to contribute to the development and management of critical educational data, which plays a significant role in shaping the policies and strategies that guide the education sector in Uganda. Over the years, I have honed my skills in analyzing complex datasets, ensuring their accuracy and relevance in supporting key decision-making processes. My work has involved supporting government agencies, researchers, and policymakers with evidence-based insights that help allocate resources efficiently, monitor educational progress, and improve the quality of education across the country. I have had the opportunity to lead and collaborate on various national surveys and educational research projects, each of which has provided valuable insights into the status of education in Uganda. These experiences have not only helped shape the Ministry’s planning and policy-making but also given me the chance to mentor and train others in the field. I have worked closely with teams at all levels to foster a culture of data-driven decision-making and to ensure that the statistics we work with are used to bring about meaningful change. My role also involves capacity building within the Ministry. I take great pride in equipping my colleagues with the tools and knowledge they need to succeed in their work, whether that involves training in statistical methodologies or guiding them through data collection and analysis processes. Through these efforts, I strive to create an environment where accurate data and sound analysis lead to positive educational outcomes for our country. It has been a rewarding journey, and I am proud to continue contributing to Uganda’s education sector, helping to improve the lives of students and communities across the nation through the power of data.
